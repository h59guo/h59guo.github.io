C vs C++: Coding vs debuging


I learned C and C++ at school, the University of Waterloo, from CS 136 and CS 246 respectively. I got an A on CS 136, which is about C, and CS 246, which is about C++, is in progress.


Talking about C, it is really a magical language, where you have to do everything on your own. (I think that's why C programmers are usually kind of arrogant XD). However, learning C did help me a lot in terms of understanding the memory model (stack, heap, etc), control flow of a program (if statement, all kinds of loops, etc), and most importantly, Abstract Data Type.


ADT is the most real world programming I learned in C, and I developed a good idea of how they works and how to implement them thanks to CS 136. I implemented about 10 ADTs myself, as a part of the assignments with very good feedback, and the TA was even impressed because they were not only bug free but also well documented.

As a CS student studying at the University of Waterloo, I have three to five assignments due each week.
